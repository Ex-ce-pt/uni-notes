
## Amino acids

(AA)

Same general structure - amino group ($\ce{-NH2}$) + carboxylic group ($\ce{-COOH}$)
```smiles
NC(C(=O)(O))[R]
```

Each has a **full name**, **short name** (3 letters) and **1 letter label** (for encoding).

#### Essential amino acids

9 of the amino acids are essential for humans and cannot be synthesised.
They have to be acquired by eating other organic matter.
List: **H, I, L, K, M, F, T, W, V**

#### Stereochemistry

Each amino acids except for Glycine is chiral.
- L-amino acids → proteins.
- D-amino acids → bacterial cell walls.

Stereochemistry is important for how proteins fold.

#### Ionization

All amino acids, by definition, have ionizable groups.

> Physiological pH = $7.4$

In physiological pH most amino acids are **zwitterionic** ($\ce{-COO^-, -NH3+}$).

Sometimes it is possible to separate proteins using isoelectric points (?)

Different groups in different amino acids have different pKas because the structure is different.

The inside of folded proteins usually contains very little water, so the concept of pKa becomes fuzzy and other reasoning applies.

#### Polarity

- Polar groups → **hydrophilic** → the outside of proteins
- Nonpolar groups - **hydrophobic** → the inside of proteins

The outside of the proteins is mostly water, so the hydrophilic groups interact with it.

#### Modifications

Amino acids can be modified after translation:
- Addition of groups: $\ce{-OH}$, $\ce{-CH3}$, $\ce{-PO4^{3-}}$
- Glycosylation in sugars
- Ubiquitination - signal for degradation, the protein will be recycled
- Lipidation
- etc.

#### Extra amino acids

**Selenocysteine** - rare, in some enzymes
**Pyrrolysine** - in archaea + some rare bacteria
