
How living organisms acquire and use energy.
Governed by thermodynamics.

Entropy of the universe increases, cells use energy to keep themselves ordered.

$\Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S$
$\Delta G < 0$ → **exergonic process** - spontaneous
$\Delta G > 0$ → **endergonic process** - nonspontaneous

Cells couple thermodynamically unfavourable reactions (e.g. synthesis) with thermodynamically favourable ones (fuel burning).

**Dynamic steady state** - the stability of the cell is continuously sustained.

**Homeostasis** - the consistency of the internal environment.

## Classification

| Acquiring energy | Source                 | Acquiring biomolecules | Source                                           |
| ---------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Phototroph       | Sunlight               | Autotroph              | Synthesises everything itself                    |
| Chemotroph       | Oxidation of chemicals | Heterotroph            | Feeds on something external to meet requirements |

Photo-/Chemo- and Auto-/Hetero- can be mixed and matched.

Examples:
- Photoautotrophs - plants.
- Chemoautotrophs - sulphur/iron/etc.-oxidising bacteria.
- Photoheterotrophs - different bacteria.
- Chemoheterotrophs - animals.
