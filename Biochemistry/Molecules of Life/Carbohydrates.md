
Polyhydroxylated hydrocarbons

Functions:
- energy source
- cell membranes
- structure
- signal transmission
- immunology - [[#Glycoproteins]]

Types:
- **Monosaccharides** - single units
- **Oligosaccharides** - several units
- **Polysaccharides** - a lot of units

#### Structure

Often monosaccharides are modified with other functional groups, e.g. **Pi**

Almost all monosaccharides have $\ce{OH}$ groups attached to asymmetric centers.
The configuration of the sugar is the configuration of the chiral center that is the farthest from the carbonyl group.

e.g. glucose has the D configuration, because $\ce{C}5$'s $\ce{OH}$ group is on the right in the Fischer projection.

All aldotetroses & monosaccharides w/ 5+ $\ce{C}$ form cycles in aqueous solutions by forming hemiacetals/hemiketals.
**Mutarotation** - interconversion of anomers in a solution: $\alpha \rightleftarrows \text{linear} \rightleftarrows \beta$.

![[carbohydrates-cyclization.png|1058]]

> 5 and 6-membered rings are always favoured!

Hemiacetal $\ce{OH}$ pointing **down** → $\alpha$ **anomer**
Hemiacetal $\ce{OH}$ pointing **up** → $\beta$ **anomer**

Linear structure → **Fischer projection**
Circular structure → **Haworth projection**

How to draw the Haworth projection:
	Points **right** (Fischer) → points **down** (Haworth)
	Points **left** (Fischer) → points **up** (Haworth)

![[glucose-fischer-haworth.png|403]]

#### Nomenclature

Carbohydrates are usually called by their historic names.
There is a convention for different types of monosaccarides.

aldehyde → aldose
ketone → ketose

$\ce{C4}$ → furan
$\ce{C5}$ → pyran

If linear structure:
	\[al/ket]\[# of $\ce{C}$]-ose
	e.g. glucose is an aldohexose
If cyclic structure:
	\[$\alpha$/$\beta$]-\[config.]-\[al/ket]\[# of $\ce{C}$]\[type of cycle]-ose
	e.g. D-glucose is an $\alpha$-D-glycopyranose or $\beta$-D-glycopyranose

## Oligosaccarides

Monosaccarides linked together.
Formed via a condensation reaction, yielding one $\ce{H2O}$ molecule.

**O-glycosidic bond** - bond between 2 monosaccarides with an $\ce{O}$ atom in the middle.

Nomenclature:
\[anomer of 1st saccaride]-1,\[$\ce{C}$ of 2nd saccaride]-glycosidic bond

e.g. maltose is 2 glucose molecules joined together by an $\alpha$-1,4-glycosidic bond

> The monosaccaride donating the hemiacetal $\ce{OH}$ cannot become linear any more!
> The last monosaccaride in the chain can become linear.

Important disaccarides:
![[important-disaccarides.png]]

> In theory, any 2 $\ce{OH}$ groups can form a glycosidic bond, but 1,4 bonds are common in nature simply because that's what nature chose over the years. 

## Polysaccarides

**Homopolysaccarides** - same monosaccaride unit.
**Heteropolysaccarides** - several different monosaccaride units.
Can be branched or unbranched.

Mainly used for cell structure and energy storage.

> Storing light metabolites in polymers means that the osmotic pressure stays low.

Summary of important ones:

|              | Cellulose       | Amylose          | Amylopectin      | Glycogen         | Chitin                      |
| ------------ | --------------- | ---------------- | ---------------- | ---------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Source**   | Plant           | Plant            | Plant            | Animal           | Animal                      |
| **Subunit**  | $\beta$-glucose | $\alpha$-glucose | $\alpha$-glucose | $\alpha$-glucose | $\beta$-N-acetylglucosamine |
| **Bonds**    | 1→4             | 1→4              | 1→4 & 1→6        | 1→4 & 1→6        | 1→4                         |
| **Branches** | ✗               | ✗                | ~per 20 subunits | ~per 10 subunits | ✗                           |

#### Amylose

**Branching:** ✗
**Repeating unit:** $\alpha$-glucose
**Links:** 1→4
**Found in:** Plant starch

![[amylose.png|767]]

#### Amylopectin

**Branching:** ✓ (occasional)
**Repeating unit:** $\alpha$-glucose
**Links:** 1→4 (in branches), 1→6 (between branches)
**Found in:** Plant starch

![[amylopectin.png|753]]

#### Glycogen

**Branching:** ✓ (fairly often)
**Repeating unit:** $\alpha$-glucose
**Links:** 1→4 (in branches), 1→6 (between branches)
**Found in:** Liver, muscles

Forms clumps visible in a microscope!

#### Cellulose

**Branching:** ✗
**Repeating unit:** $\beta$-glucose
**Links:** 1→4
**Found in:** Plants

Forms rigid fibres through $\ce{H}$ bonding between the chains.

![[cellulose.png]]

> Most abundant polysaccharide!

#### Chitin

**Branching:** ✗
**Repeating unit:** $\beta$-N-acetylglucosamine
**Links:** 1→4
**Found in:** Exoskeleton of animals

![[chitin.png]]

> 2nd most abundant polysaccharide!

## Glycoproteins

Carbohydrates covalently attached to proteins.
Often component of cell membranes.

Carbohydrates are hydrophilic → carbohydrates point outside of the protein.

**Glycan** - sugar chain attached to a protein.

**N-linked** - connected by the nitrogen of the AA side chain (e.g. Asp)
**O-linked** - connected by the oxygen of the AA side chain (e.g. Ser/Thr)

Many functions:
- pathogen binding
- self-nonself discrimination
- antibodies
- etc.

**Lectin** - protein that can recognise specific glycans.
