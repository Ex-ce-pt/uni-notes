
3 domains of life:
- Prokaryotes - no nucleus:
	- Bacteria - in soil, water, tissues, etc.
	- Archaea - unicellular, live in extreme environments.
- Eukarya:
	- Plants, animals, fungi, etc.


Bacterial cells are much smaller that animal cells.

Mainly in eukarya.
Used to separate metabolic processes within a cell.

#### Plasma membrane

Separates a cell from the environment.
Made of:
- Peptides (proteins)
- Carbohydrates

#### Cytoplasm

The internal volume of a cell.
Enclosed by the plasma membrane.

#### Cytoskeleton

Helps the inner structure of a cell.
Gives the stability.

#### Nucleus

Contains DNA in chromatin.
Where DNA **transcribed** into mRNA.
**Nuclear pores** - how mRNA gets out of the nucleus.
Has **2** membranes.

#### Ribosomes

Synthesise proteins (**translation**).

#### Mitochondrion

Only in eukarya.

Oxidizes fuels, generates ATP.
**Matrix** - the inside of a mitochondrion.
Has own DNA and ribosomes.
Has **2** membranes.
Similar to proteobacteria.

**Endosymbiont hypothesis** - mitochondria used to be bacteria that fused with eukaryotic cells.
Same thing is thought to have happened with **chloroplasts** in plants.
Enormous amount of evidence supports the theory.

#### Smooth endoplasmic reticulum (SER)

Synthesises lipids (fats).
Metabolises drugs.

#### Golgi complex

Prepares and targets packets of chemicals to other organelles or outside.
