DNA → DNA

Restriction enzyme cuts:
	**Overhead cut** - one strand is longer than the other.
	**Blunt cut** - both pieces are of the same length.


DNA replication is **semiconservative** - 1 old strand, 1 is newly synthesised.

## Terms & concepts

**Helicases** - enzymes that move along the DNA and separate the strands.
**Topoisomerases** - enzymes that relieve the topological stress from the separation.
**DNA-binding proteins** - stabilize the separated strands & protect from DNases.
**Primases** - enzymes that synthesise short segments of RNA to serve as primers.

RNase H1 - degrades RNA in RNA-DNA hybrids.
DNA ligases - seals nicks in the DNA backbone after the removal of the RNA primers.

**oriC** - replication origin; region of DNA where replication begins.

#### DNA Polymerase reaction

DNA Polymerase goes 3'→5', synthesising a 5'→3' strand.
To keep the phosphate groups in place 2 $\ce{Mg^{2+}}$ are used.
The enzyme's active site only suits the complementary base pairs.
Sometimes, mistakes happen, though.

#### Initiation phase

**DNA unwinding element (DUE)** - a region rich in $\ce{\text{A}=\text{T}}$ pairs.

**DnaA** binds to the replication origin and wraps it around itself.
The stress snaps open the helix at the DUE that is located nearby.
A replication fork is created.

**Replication fork** - where the DNA is being unwound and separated.

**DnaB** (**helicase**) binds to the strands and opens up the DNA further.
**Needs ATP.**

**DnaC** - coenzyme for DnaB.

> More genes - more replication origins.

> The single-stranded DNA must be protected at all times by the **DNA-binding proteins**!

Replication can go either in a single direction or in both directions (usually).

#### Elongation phase

Initially DNA is twisted (helix).
**Topoisomerase I** breaks one of the strands and swaps them around to create 2 linear segments parallel to each other; separates the strands from one another.
**Doesn't need ATP.**

![[_media/Information Pathways/DNA Replication/topoisomerase-work.png]]

**Topoisomerase II** - relaxes the strain.
**Needs ATP.**

![[_media/Information Pathways/DNA Replication/replication-addition-of-nucleotide.png]]

**Primases** synthesise small RNA primers to guide the polymerase to where it needs to start the replication.

If polymerase notices a mistake, it removes the last few nucleotides and synthesises them again.

**Leading strand** - one continuously replicated strand.
**Lagging strand** - replicated in short segments.
**Okazaki fragments** - short segments of the lagging strand that are synthesised.
For the lagging strand, the DNA Polymerase must continuously just back some nucleotides and synthesise a new Okazaki fragment.

Replication fork moves ~50 nucleotides/second (eukaryotic)

![[_media/Information Pathways/DNA Replication/replication-elongation.png]]

#### Termination phase

First, RNase removes all the RNA primers.
A different DNA Polymerase fills the leftover gaps.
Then, DNA ligase fills all the gaps in the phosphate backbone.

**Tus protein** - only allows the fork to pass in one direction

The strands are resolved and then detached.

![[_media/Information Pathways/DNA Replication/replication-termination-resolution.png]]

---

The last segment of the lagging strand is not replicated - no primer synthesised!
In each division 30-200 bases are lost.

**Telomere** - the end of the DNA.
**Telomerases** - DNA Polymerase that adds the last part of the lagging strand.

#### Other ways

Mitochondria use the rolling circle method.
Circular DNA is cut, rolled out into a linear strand, and then copied.

#### Error correction

The old DNA strand is methylated, the newly synthesised one is not.
When a mistake is found, the methylated nucleotide is assumed to be the correct one.

Types of errors:
- Base pair mismatch
- Chemicals trapped within
- Breaks in the strands
- Thymine dimers

**Substitution mutation** - ABC → AOC
**Insertion mutation** - ABC → ABOC
**Deletion mutation** - ABC → AC

~200 genes encode proteins for DNA repair.

One base pair doesn't get removed, a whole chunk is cut out and synthesised again.
