proton gradient alone is enough to power atp synthesis

2 functional domains:
F0 - in the inner membrane
F1 - poking out of the inner membrane, looks like a mushroom, catalytic domain
alpha and beta subunits form an alternating ring
gamma forms a long stalk in the center
F0 is a ring of c protein

atp synthesis is in beta of F1
asymmetric interaction with gamma is crucial

beta conformations:

## ETC

Sequential flow of e over 4 complexes
moves protons from inside to intermembrane space, then letting them enter to power atp synthase

protonmotive force - energy from protons spontaneously moving from one place to another

cristae - folds of the inner membrane of a mitochondrion

outer membrane is very porous
inner membrane is very selective

low [H+] within the matrix
high [H+] outside
strong electrochemical gradient

> The e flow through the 4 complexes are the ETC, but the ATP synthesis is not - it's oxidative phosphorylation!


#### Complex I

34 subunits

e flow: NADH -> FMN -> Q

pumps 4 H+ out of the matrix

P side - intermembrane space
N side - mitochondrial matrix space

FMN - flavin mononucleotide

flavins can take only one e at a time, forming radicals
it is important, because the next steps can only accept 1 e at a time

Cys-S-Fe-...-Fe-S-Cys complexes act as nanowires, transporting e

Q - ubiquinone, a lipid
QH2 freely diffuses in the inner membrane

Ovrall reaction: NADH + 5 H+_N + Q -> NAD+ + QH2 + 4 H+_P

#### Complex II

SDH - succinate dehydrogenase

oxidizes succinate, FAD -> FADH2
e transferred one by one via Fe-S wires
again, FADH2 + Q -> FAD+ + QH2

does not pump any protons!

#### Complex III

Functions:
- transfers e from QH2 to protein cytochrome c
- pumps more H+

cytochromes are protiens that have heme groups
cytochrome c is diffuseable  and can move around
can accept 1 e at a time, reducing Fe3+ to Fe2+

Steps:
- Q cycle - does 1 e movement

1 e goes to cytochrome c
1 e goes to Q, creating a Q.- radical
Q.- + 2 H+ -> QH2, which goes back into the cycle

#### Complex IV

cytochrome c transports e to O2, forming H2O
dimer of 13 subunits, 26 subunits in total
contains Cu2+

Cu_a center
Cu_b associated with heme a3

---

summary: 2 NADH + 22 H+_n + O2 -> 2NAD+ + 20 H+_P + 2 H2O

FADH2 bypasses complex i

respirasome - supercomplexes of complexes I, III, and IV for efficiency and entrapment of toxic reactive oxygen species
that's why oxygen can be toxic and cause dna damage!

respirasomes are on the flat parts of the cristae
atp synthase is on the folds on cristae

complex II is free floating
