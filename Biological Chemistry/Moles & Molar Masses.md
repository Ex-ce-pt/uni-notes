## Moles

1 mole = # of atoms in 12 g of carbon-12.
$1 \space mole = 6.0332 \times 10^{23} \space objects$

$n = \frac{N}{N_A}$

| Symbol | Unit       | Explanation                                |
| ------ | ---------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| $n$    | $mol$      | Chemical amount of substance               |
| $N$    | particles  | # of particles                             |
| $N_A$  | $mol^{-1}$ | [Avogadro's constant](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Constants) |

> Be careful, make sure to count the right objects (atoms/molecules/formula units)!

## Molar Mass

Mass per mole of objects.

$n=\frac{m}{M}$

| Symbol | Unit               | Explanation                  |
| ------ | ------------------ | ---------------------------- |
| $n$    | $mol$              | Chemical amount of substance |
| $m$    | $g$                | Mass of the sample           |
| $M$    | $g \cdot mol^{-1}$ | Molar mass of the substance  |

Molar mass of atoms is determined through the masses of individual atoms (and those through mass spectrometry):

$M = m_{atom}N_A$

| Symbol     | Unit               | Explanation                                |
| ---------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ |
| $M$        | $g \cdot mol^{-1}$ | Molar mass of the substance                |
| $m_{atom}$ | $g$                | Mass of a single atom                      |
| $N_A$      | $mol^{-1}$         | [Avogadro's constant](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Constants) |

Elements have a lot of isotopes, so the average mass of an atom is calculated:

$m_{atom, avg}=\sum_{isotopes} f_{isotope}m_{isotope}$

| Symbol          | Unit | Explanation                        |
| --------------- | ---- | ---------------------------------- |
| $m_{atom, avg}$ | $g$  | Average mass of a single atom      |
| $f_{isotope}$   | -    | Fractional abundance of an isotope |
| $m_{isotope}$   | $g$  | Mass of an isotope                 |
**Atomic weight** = molar mass of an atom.
**Molecular weight/formula weight** = molar mass of a molecule/formula unit.
