
**Matter** - anything that has mass and takes up space.
**Substance** - single, pure form of matter.
**State of matter** - a form of matter.

**Solid** - retains shape, doesn't flow.
**Liquid** - has a well-defined surface, takes the shape of a part of the vessel.
**Gas** - fills any vessel.
**Vapor** - gaseous form of a substance that's normally solid or liquid.

**Physical property** - observable and measurable without changing the identity of the substance.
**Chemical property** - ability to be changed into another substance.

**Extensive property** - depends on the size of the sample (eg. mass, volume).
**Intensive property** - doesn't depend on the size of the sample (eg. density, temperature).

## Force

Force - influence that changes the state of motion of an object.
Eg. electromagnetic.

$\vec F = m \vec a$

| Symbol   | Unit             | Explanation                                                |
| -------- | ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| $\vec F$ | $N$              | Force vector applied to the object (colinear to $\vec a$ ) |
| $m$      | $kg$             | Mass for the object                                        |
| $\vec a$ | $m \cdot s^{-2}$ | Acceleration vector of the object                          |

[Unit](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Units): $[F] = \text{Mass} \times \text{Distance} \times \text{Time}^{-2} = kg \cdot m \cdot s^{-2} = N (\text{newton})$

Acceleration: $\vec a = \frac{d \vec v}{dt} = \frac{d^2x}{dt^2}$
Unit: $[a] = \text{Distance} \times \text{Time}^{-2} = m \cdot s^{-2}$

Speed: $\vec v = \frac{dx}{dt}$
Unit: $[v] = \text{Distance} \times \text{Time}^{-1} = m \cdot s^{-1}$

## Energy

Energy - capacity to do work.

$E = Fs = |\vec F|s$

| Symbol | Unit | Explanation                                                 |
| ------ | ---- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| $E$    | $J$  | Energy of the object                                        |
| $F$    | $N$  | Force (magnitude of the force vector) applied to the object |
| $s$    | $m$  | Distance the object travels with the force applied          |

[Unit](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Units): $[E] = \text{Mass} \times \text{Distance}^2 \times \text{Time}^{-2} = kg \cdot m^2 \cdot s^{-2} = J (\text{joule})$

Kinetic energy ($E_k$) - energy of motion.
$E_k = \frac{1}{2} mv^2$

| Symbol | Unit             | Explanation                  |
| ------ | ---------------- | ---------------------------- |
| $E_K$  | $J$              | Kinetic energy of the object |
| $m$    | $kg$             | Mass of the object           |
| $v$    | $m \cdot s^{-1}$ | Speed of the object          |

Potential energy ($E_p$) - energy on account of the position of the object in a field of force.
Formula depends on the force field.

Gravitational:
$E_p = mgh$

| Symbol | Unit             | Explanation                                      |
| ------ | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| $E_p$  | $J$              | Potential energy of the object                   |
| $m$    | $kg$             | Mass of the object                               |
| $g$    | $m \cdot s^{-2}$ | [Acceleration of free fall](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Constants) |
| $h$    | $m$              | Height the object is at                          |

Coulomb:
$E_p = \frac{Q_1Q_2}{4 \pi \epsilon_0 r}$

| Symbol       | Unit                            | Explanation                                |
| ------------ | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| $E_p$        | $J$                             | Potential energy of the object             |
| $Q_1$        | $C$                             | Charge of particle 1                       |
| $Q_2$        | $C$                             | Charge of particle 2                       |
| $\epsilon_0$ | $J^{-1} \cdot C^2 \cdot m^{-1}$ | [Vacuum permittivity](Cheat%20Sheet.md#Constants) |
| $r$          | $m$                             | Distance between the charges               |

#### Conservation of energy

In a closed system, the total energy stays constant.
$E = E_k + E_p$
